I chose this photo because the Petronas Twin Towers are an iconic landmark of Malaysia, and I find them very interesting. This is a photo I took during my first visit there.

To model the sign appearing in the photo, I first determined that its shape closely resembles an absolute value function, so I chose the parent function y = |x| as my starting point.

By observing the direction of the angle formed by the two rays in the image, I noticed that it opens downward rather than upward. This indicates a reflection across the x-axis, so I modified the function to y = -|x| to match the orientation of the graph.

Domain and Range

The domain of my function is D:{x∈R | −8≤x≤0}. I limited the domain to the portion of the graph visible in the photograph.

The parent function y = |x| has a domain of all real numbers and a range of y ≥ 0. After applying the transformations, the graph opens downward because of the reflection across the x-axis.

Therefore, the range of the function is approximately: R:{y∈R | 9.90≤y≤15.80}
